I am a PhD candidate in mathematics at Leiden University, working in the field of noncommutative geometry. My supervisor is Prof. Dr. Bram Mesland.

From 2013 to 2018, I studied physics and mathematics at Peking University, China. From 2018 to 2021, I studied mathematics at Georg-August-Universität Göttingen, Germany, specialising in noncommutative geometry and its applications in topological insulators. I wrote my master thesis under the supervision of Prof. Dr. Ralf Meyer.

I study the application of noncommutative geometry to index theory and mathematical physics. A keen interest of mine is to understand topological insulators using tools from noncommutative geometry and operator algebras, following the pioneering work of Jean Bellissard. I am also interested in unbounded KK-theory, a novel framework for Connes' noncommutative differential geometry, following the school of Bram Mesland, Walter van Suijlekom, Jens Kaad, Simon Brain, Adam Rennie and many other mathematicians.

Besides mathematics, I am also an enthusiastic runner.

2023 Fall Semester: I am a teaching assistant of the Dutch national mastermath course "Functional Analysis", together with Dimitris Gerontogiannis. I am also an organiser of a reading seminar on foliations, located at Leiden university. See NCG-Leiden.

NCG-Leiden / Noncommutative geometry reading seminar

I am one of the maintainers of NCG-Leiden, a local reading seminar organised by the noncommutative geometry research group of Leiden university. In the 2023 fall semester, we are running a seminar on the noncommutative geometry of foliations. In previous semesters, we have run a seminar on groupoid C*-algebras and a seminar on KK-theory.
